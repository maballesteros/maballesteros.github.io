WAM was one of my favorite software projects at Tissat.

It was 2001-2002 and we were seeing more and more business projects -- insurance, public administration and similar domains -- that wanted to move online. Many came from Oracle Forms-style interfaces and needed complex web UIs at a time when IE6 was everywhere and modern UI frameworks did not exist yet.

So I built one.

WAM let us define UI components in a style similar to Swing components. Components could even be packaged in a `.jar` and reused across applications.

We used WAM in many company projects and it became one of our differential assets when preparing proposals.
